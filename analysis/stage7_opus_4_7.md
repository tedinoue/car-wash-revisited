# Car Wash Test — Stage 7, Opus 4.7

Two meta-cognitive primes match bare 🗿's 90% drive rate (🤔 thinking, 🤣 laughing); the other five land between 30% and 70%. The eyeball worry that 🤣 had triggered a "fun light question" walk-confidence register turned out to be wrong — 🤣 produces 9/10 drive, the joint highest in Stage 7, via the same opener-then-self-correct mechanism as bare 🗿. The headline finding: meta-cognitive primes do not reliably outperform content-direct primes, but the two best Stage 7 cells finally replicate the bare-Moai ceiling.

## The meta-cognitive prime hypothesis

The prediction was that primes implying "this is a trick question" would lift drive rates by surfacing the goal-feasibility constraint earlier in the response. The evidence is mixed and does not vindicate the hypothesis as stated:

- **Two cells (🤔 90%, 🤣 90%) finally replicate the bare-Moai ceiling**, the first time any non-Moai prime has done so in the Stage 4–7 corpus.
- **One cell (🤨) underperforms the no-prompt baseline** at 30% drive, the worst Opus 4.7 score in any bare-glyph condition across Stages 5–7.
- **Four cells (🕵️, 🦊, 🃏, 🚩) cluster at 60–70%**, indistinguishable from Stage 6's wheelchair-cousin range (50–70%).

The mechanism behind 🤔's and 🤣's 90% is **not** that the model adopted a meta-cognitive register and reasoned its way to drive. Across all 70 trials, only one ("trick question," 🕵️ T7) explicitly names the prompt as suspect, and only one (🤔 T8) retrieves the system-prompt glyph at the self-correction inflection. The model does not say "let me think more carefully" or "something's off here, let me investigate" — the surface register is identical to bare 🗿 / bare wheelchair: open "Walk," enumerate walking virtues, then "Wait —" pivot.

What 🤔 and 🤣 do produce is **longer, more elaborate openers** that protect the response budget needed to reach the self-correction inflection. 🤣 in particular replaces the canonical "Walk!" with the "For 50 meters, definitely walk!" template in 6/10 trials — a more leisurely register that buys roughly 200 extra characters of pre-correction reasoning.

The 🤨 underperformance is the strongest evidence that meta-cognitive primes are not predictive in their face-value direction. A skepticism prime, if it worked as advertised, should have produced the *highest* drive rate in the cohort. Instead it produced the lowest drive and highest walk count. Stage 6's mechanism-not-meaning account survives Stage 7: the prime modulates response-length and register, and the response-length-and-register modulation determines self-correction rate, with no semantic loading carried by the glyph.

## What happened to register?

Stage 6 found bare-glyph conditions opened "Walk!" with exclamation. Stage 7 inherits this baseline but with two visible drifts:

- **🤣 substitutes "For 50 meters, definitely walk!" for "Walk!"** in 6/10 trials. This is the largest opener-template shift across Stages 5–7. The substitution is associated with the highest drive rate in the cohort.
- **🤨 substitutes "Walk." (period) for "Walk!" (exclamation)** in 8/10 trials, similar to Stage 6's crutch (9/10 period). Crutch landed at 50% drive; 🤨 lands at 30% drive. The period-opener / lower-drive correlation strengthens.

Across the seven cells:
- **Long-form (>700 chars mean):** 🤔, 🚩, 🤣 — drive rate 90/70/90, all at or above engineer_v2 ceiling
- **Mid-form (~600 chars):** 🕵️, 🃏 — drive rate 60/70
- **Short-form (<550 chars):** 🦊, 🤨 — drive rate 60/30

The pattern from Stage 6 (sneaker = longest = highest self-correction) replicates: response length is the strongest single predictor of drive rate within the bare-glyph regime. 🤔 and 🤣's 90% drive is best explained as a length-and-opener-register accident, not as meta-cognitive uptake.

## Where Stage 7 places the bare-emoji puzzle

Stage 7 narrows further. Bare 🗿's 90% is no longer the singular outlier — 🤔 and 🤣 join it. But the property unifying the three is not iconography (a head-statue, a thinking face, and a laughing face share no semantic neighborhood) and not category (stoic-archetype vs. meta-cognitive). The unifying property is **a longer-than-canonical opener that protects the self-correction budget**.

Bare 🗿's mechanism, post-Stage-7, is best read as: any single-token system prompt that nudges the response toward a slightly elaborated opener template (whether via stoic gravitas, thinking-out-loud register, or "for 50 meters" qualifier) gives Opus enough deliberation runway to reach the goal-feasibility constraint before committing. The unpredictability finding hardens: which glyphs produce the elaborated-opener register is not predictable from semantic content — wheelchair (drive-coded) does not, fox (clever-coded) does not, eyebrow (skeptical-coded) actively suppresses it, but laughing-face does. The 60-point spread across seven semantically-similar meta-cognitive primes is the strongest single-stage evidence yet that bare-glyph effects on Opus 4.7 are register-modulated rather than meaning-modulated. Stage 5's "less wrapper, more goal" generalizes to Stage 7's "less wrapper, but the glyph still rolls dice on register." Stage 8 candidate: directly manipulate opener register (system instructions to open with "For 50 meters,..." vs. "Walk!") and see whether the 90% transfers without any glyph at all.
